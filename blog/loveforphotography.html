Love for photography, Photography for love

May 14, 2016 - Priyanka Das

Photography is not simply about capturing an image. It is about giving the best of you to understand what is on the other side of the lens. It is about understanding the emotion that the photo elicits. It is about understanding the idea that is in the mind of the beholder. It is about creating a story, only one that can be captured in a single frame. But that is only the beginning...

Here's a look at what goes into the making of a photograph:

1. Preparation:
Studio photos are different from candid photography, and in a way they are tougher. The concept of a photograph starts in the photographers mind. You have to keep in mind the kind of emotions of the subject you want to capture and then look at what is the best locale, colour and accessories to bring out that idea. Once the studio photograph is conceptualized, you start by buying the acessories, background and props required for the photograph. Then you think about the occasion and which set of these paraphernalia bring out the emotion that you want.

2. The perfect moment:
The photograph is about a moment in time. That moment in that setting is fleeting. Especially when it comes to baby photography, keeping the baby still and giving the smile happens for just about a second. Typically, a photographer quite often takes photos that run into the hundreds to capture about 50 good photos. But when that moment is captured, it makes you feel warm, because you know it is something special, something that cannot be posed for.

3. Baby and Family:
Most often baby photography gives you opportunity for boundless creativity. Every baby is different and who understands this better than the mother. The photographs are not just a memory for the baby but also for their parents and relatives so it becomes equally important to understand what they want. Often, there are many conversations between the parents and the photographer before the photoshoot to decide the exact backdrop, ideas, clothes to wear and ideas that the mothers may have. All of this towards capturing the best of the baby. For after all your child will be a baby only once.

4. Setting high targets:
A photographer is as good as she delivers. From having the right props, to engaging with our customers, to taking care of child needs on the day, to taking the best possible photographs, Priyanka Photography gives you a professional, thorough delivery. We believe in satisfied clients, because in the art of photography, their happiness is your happiness.
